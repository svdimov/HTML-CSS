A Little History of the
World Wide Web

  1. March 1989

    Tim laid out his vision for what would become the web in a document called “Information Management: A Proposal”. Believe it or not, Tim’s initial proposal was not immediately accepted. His boss Mike Sendall noted the words “Vague but exciting”.

  2. October of 1990

    Tim had written the three foundational technologies of today’s web: HTML, URI (URL), and HTTP.

  3. March 1989

    Tim laid out his vision for what would become the web in a document called “Information Management: A Proposal”…

  4. End of 1990

    The first web page was served on the open internet.

  5. 1991

    People outside of CERN were invited to join this new web community.

  6. 1994

    Tim moved from CERN to MIT to found the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), devoted to open web standards.
